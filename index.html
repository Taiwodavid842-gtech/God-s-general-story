God's General Story
George Müller
The Man Who Trusted God for Everything
George Müller is remembered as one of the greatest examples of faith and prayer in Christian history. Through a life of complete dependence on God, he cared for thousands of orphaned children without asking people for financial support. His story continues to inspire believers to trust God's faithfulness. Early Life George Müller was born on September 27, 1805, in Kroppenstedt. As a boy, George was intelligent but rebellious. He often lied, stole money, and lived a careless life. Even when his mother was dying, he showed little concern and continued his sinful lifestyle. His father hoped he would become a minister—not because George loved God, but because it offered financial security. His Conversion While studying at the University of Halle, a friend invited George to a Christian Bible study. At first, he attended out of curiosity. That evening, he saw ordinary Christians praying with sincerity and joy. Their love for God deeply touched him. He later wrote that he had never before seen people pray with such genuine faith. That night, George surrendered his life to Jesus Christ. From then on, everything changed. A New Life After his conversion, Müller devoted himself to: Reading the Bible daily. Spending time in prayer. Sharing the Gospel. Living by faith. He gave up his dishonest ways and sought to honor God in every area of his life. Called to England God led George to England, where he became a pastor. While serving in Bristol, he noticed the terrible condition of orphaned children. Many had no homes, no food, and no one to care for them. George believed God wanted him to help—not only to care for the children but also to demonstrate that God answers prayer. Building the Orphanages In 1836, Müller opened his first orphanage. He made an unusual decision: He would never ask anyone for money. Instead, he would tell God every need in prayer and trust Him to provide. Over the years, God supplied everything needed through unexpected donations and generous supporters. Eventually, Müller established orphanages that cared for more than 10,000 children during his lifetime. He also helped educate thousands of children and distributed millions of Bibles and Christian books. Miracles of Provision Many remarkable stories are told about Müller's ministry. One morning, the children sat at the breakfast table with no food. Instead of worrying, Müller thanked God for the meal they did not yet have. Moments later, a baker knocked on the door, saying God had impressed him during the night to bake bread for the orphanage. Soon after, a milk cart broke down outside the orphanage. Rather than let the milk spoil, the driver gave it to the children. To Müller, these were not coincidences but answers to prayer. His Prayer Life George Müller kept careful records of his prayers. During his lifetime, he recorded more than 50,000 specific answers to prayer, many of them answered within hours or days. He believed that prayer should be based on God's promises in Scripture and offered with faith, patience, and submission to God's will. Personal Challenges Although Müller trusted God deeply, his life was not free from sorrow. 1. A Sinful Past He had to leave behind a life of dishonesty and selfishness after his conversion. 2. Financial Pressure There were many days when the orphanages had no visible means of support. Yet Müller refused to panic, choosing instead to pray. 3. Loss of Loved Ones His first wife, Mary Groves Müller, whom he loved dearly, died after years of faithful ministry. Her death caused him deep grief, but he continued trusting God's goodness. 4. Heavy Responsibility Caring for thousands of children required constant dependence on God. Missionary Travels After the orphanages were well established, Müller traveled extensively to preach. He visited more than 40 countries, encouraging believers to trust God and live by faith. Even in his seventies and eighties, he continued preaching with remarkable energy. His Final Years George Müller remained active in ministry until the end of his life. He died peacefully on March 10, 1898, at the age of 92. By then, he had become a living testimony that God is faithful to provide for those who trust Him. Lessons from George Müller's Life 1. God Answers Prayer Prayer is powerful because God is faithful. 2. Trust God Completely Even when resources seem impossible, God is able to provide. 3. Live by Faith, Not by Fear Faith grows as we rely on God's promises. 4. Care for Those in Need True Christianity expresses itself through practical love and compassion. 5. Persevere in God's Work Müller remained faithful through joy, loss, and uncertainty. Conclusion George Müller's life proves that God is still faithful to those who trust Him completely. From a rebellious young man to a servant who cared for thousands of orphans, his journey was marked by unwavering faith, persistent prayer, and complete dependence on God. His legacy continues to encourage believers to seek God first, pray with confidence, and trust that the God who provided for George Müller is the same God who still provides today.
