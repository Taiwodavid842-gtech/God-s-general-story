God's General Story
Maria Woodworth-Etter
The Evangelist Who Preached with Bold Faith
Maria Woodworth-Etter was one of the pioneering female evangelists in American history. She became widely known for her passionate preaching, calls to repentance, and large revival meetings. Many people also associated her ministry with reports of healing and other extraordinary spiritual experiences, though accounts of these events have been viewed differently by various Christian traditions. Early Life Maria Beulah Woodworth was born on July 22, 1844, in Lisbon, Ohio. She grew up in a Christian family during a period of spiritual revival in America. From a young age, she sensed that God was calling her into ministry. At a time when it was uncommon for women to preach publicly, Maria felt a strong conviction that God wanted her to proclaim the Gospel. Marriage and Family Maria married Philo Woodworth in 1867. Together they had several children. Tragically, many of their children died at young ages from illness. These heartbreaking losses tested Maria's faith deeply. Instead of turning away from God, she became more devoted to prayer and ministry. Her marriage also faced difficulties because her husband did not fully support her growing evangelistic calling. They eventually separated. Years later, she married Samuel Etter, after which she became known as Maria Woodworth-Etter. Answering God's Call For several years, Maria struggled with whether to preach because of opposition and fear. She later wrote that delaying obedience left her spiritually burdened. Eventually, she surrendered completely to God's call. When she began preaching, many people responded to the Gospel, and revival meetings quickly grew. A Ministry of Revival Maria traveled across the United States, holding revival services in churches, tents, and open fields. Her preaching focused on: Repentance from sin. Faith in Jesus Christ. Holiness. The power of the Holy Spirit. Prayer and dependence on God. Thousands attended her meetings, and many testified that their lives were transformed. Reports of Healing Maria's ministry became especially known for reports of divine healing. Many attendees testified that they experienced physical healing after prayer. Others described powerful spiritual experiences during her meetings. While many believers viewed these as genuine works of God, some contemporaries questioned or disputed certain reports. Her ministry remains an important part of the history of the early Pentecostal and Holiness movements. Throughout her ministry, Maria consistently encouraged people to place their faith in Jesus Christ rather than in any human preacher. Challenges and Opposition Maria faced significant criticism. Some questioned whether a woman should preach. Others criticized the unusual events reported at her meetings. Despite this opposition, she continued preaching because she believed God had called her to proclaim the Gospel. She often reminded believers that obedience to God is more important than seeking human approval. Her Influence Maria's ministry helped prepare the way for later Pentecostal evangelists. Many Christian leaders were influenced by her emphasis on prayer, revival, and faith in God's power. Her boldness also encouraged other women to answer God's call to ministry. Her Final Years Maria continued preaching well into her seventies. She died on September 16, 1924, leaving behind a legacy of evangelism, prayer, and revival. Lessons from Maria Woodworth-Etter's Life 1. Obey God's Calling Even when others doubted her, Maria chose to obey what she believed God had called her to do. 2. God Can Bring Hope Through Suffering The loss of her children and other personal hardships deepened her dependence on God rather than destroying her faith. 3. Prayer Is Powerful Her ministry was rooted in prayer and reliance on God. 4. Share the Gospel Boldly She preached Christ with courage despite criticism and opposition. 5. Give God the Glory Maria consistently pointed people to Jesus as the source of salvation and healing. Memorable Thought Maria Woodworth-Etter often emphasized that God is still able to save, heal, and transform lives, and she urged people to trust Christ wholeheartedly. Conclusion Maria Woodworth-Etter's life was marked by both deep personal sorrow and remarkable ministry. She overcame grief, opposition, and cultural barriers to become one of America's most influential evangelists. Her story reminds us that God can use those who faithfully obey Him, even in difficult circumstances, to bring hope and the message of Christ to many people.
