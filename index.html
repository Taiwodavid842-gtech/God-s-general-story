God's General Story
John G. Lake
The Missionary Who Trusted God for Healing and the Power of the Holy Spirit
John G. Lake was one of the most influential Pentecostal leaders of the early twentieth century. He is remembered for his passionate preaching, missionary work in Southern Africa, and his emphasis on faith, prayer, and the power of the Holy Spirit. Early Life John Graham Lake was born on March 18, 1870, in St. Marys. His family later moved to the United States, where he grew up. Lake came from a large family, and several of his brothers and sisters suffered from serious illnesses. These experiences deeply affected him and stirred his desire to understand God's power to heal. As a young man, he worked successfully in business and became financially prosperous. His Conversion John G. Lake gave his life to Jesus Christ as a young man and became actively involved in church ministry. As he studied the Bible, he became convinced that God still heals today, just as He did in the New Testament. One experience that strengthened his faith was the reported healing of his wife, Jennie, after she had suffered from severe illness. Lake believed this answered prayer confirmed God's healing power. A Life-Changing Call Although Lake enjoyed success in business, he sensed God calling him into full-time ministry. After much prayer, he chose to leave behind his business career and dedicate his life to preaching the Gospel. It was a costly decision, but he believed obeying God was worth more than earthly wealth. Receiving the Holy Spirit Lake became associated with the early Pentecostal movement and testified that he experienced the baptism in the Holy Spirit. He believed this empowered him for evangelism, missions, and prayer for the sick. From then on, his ministry became increasingly focused on proclaiming Christ and encouraging believers to trust God. Missionary to South Africa In 1908, John G. Lake traveled with a missionary team to South Africa. The work grew rapidly. He preached in towns and villages, planted churches, trained local leaders, and emphasized prayer and discipleship. Many people testified to spiritual renewal, conversions, and healing during his ministry. His efforts contributed significantly to the growth of Pentecostal Christianity in Southern Africa. Personal Challenges John G. Lake's life included many painful trials. 1. Leaving Wealth Behind He gave up a successful business career to follow God's call into ministry. 2. The Death of His Wife While serving in South Africa, his wife, Jennie Lake, died after a period of illness. Her death brought deep sorrow, yet Lake continued serving God. 3. Opposition Some people questioned his teachings and ministry, while others criticized reports of healing associated with his work. Despite criticism, he remained committed to preaching the Gospel. 4. Demands of Missionary Life Travel, limited resources, and difficult conditions made missionary work physically and emotionally demanding. Healing Rooms After returning to the United States, Lake established Healing Rooms in Spokane. These were places where people could receive prayer and pastoral care. Many testimonies of healing came from this ministry, although such reports are understood differently across Christian traditions. Lake always emphasized that Jesus Christ—not any individual minister—is the source of healing. His Teachings John G. Lake taught that: Jesus Christ is the same yesterday, today, and forever. Believers should live by faith in God's promises. The Holy Spirit empowers Christians for service. Prayer is essential to the Christian life. The Gospel should be accompanied by compassion and practical ministry. His Final Years John G. Lake continued preaching, teaching, and mentoring ministers until his health declined. He died on September 16, 1935, at the age of 65. His influence continued through the churches he planted, the leaders he trained, and his writings. Lessons from John G. Lake's Life 1. Obey God's Call God's purpose is worth more than worldly success. 2. Trust God Through Trials Faith is tested and strengthened during difficult seasons. 3. Depend on the Holy Spirit The Christian life is lived through God's power, not human ability. 4. Pray Boldly Bring every need before God with confidence in His wisdom and goodness. 5. Share the Gospel with Compassion Ministry involves both proclaiming Christ and caring for people. Conclusion John G. Lake's life demonstrates the impact of wholehearted obedience to God. He left behind financial success to become a missionary, endured personal loss and opposition, and devoted himself to preaching Christ and encouraging believers to trust in God's power. His story continues to inspire Christians to live by faith, seek the guidance of the Holy Spirit, and remain faithful to God's calling regardless of the cost.
