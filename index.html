God's General Story
Mary Slessor
The Missionary Who Brought the Love of Christ to Nigeria
Mary Slessor is remembered as one of the greatest missionaries in Christian history. She devoted nearly 40 years of her life to serving the people of what is now southeastern Nigeria. Through her courage, compassion, and unwavering faith, she preached the Gospel, cared for the helpless, promoted peace among communities, and fought against harmful cultural practices, including the killing of twins. Early Life Mary Mitchell Slessor was born on December 2, 1848, in Aberdeen. Her family was poor, and her father struggled with alcoholism. As a result, Mary began working in a textile mill when she was only 11 years old to help support her family. Despite the hardships, her mother taught her to love God and read the Bible. Mary developed a deep passion for prayer and missionary work. A Burden for Africa As a young woman, Mary was greatly inspired by the stories of missionary David Livingstone, who had served in Africa. After Livingstone's death, Mary felt that God was calling her to continue sharing the Gospel in Africa. She prayed earnestly for the opportunity to become a missionary. Journey to Nigeria In 1876, at the age of 27, Mary arrived in Calabar under the mission of the United Presbyterian Church of Scotland. Life was difficult. The tropical climate, unfamiliar language, diseases, and challenging travel conditions made missionary work dangerous. Many missionaries became seriously ill, but Mary trusted God and remained faithful. Living Among the People Unlike many foreigners of her time, Mary chose to live among the local people. She learned their language, respected their culture where it did not conflict with the Gospel, and built genuine friendships. This helped her earn the trust of the communities she served. She traveled to remote villages that many others were afraid to enter. Rescuing Abandoned Twins One of Mary's best-known ministries involved rescuing twins. In some communities, twins were wrongly believed to bring bad luck. As a result, many twin babies were abandoned or killed, and their mothers were often rejected. Mary courageously challenged this practice. She rescued many abandoned twins, cared for them, and taught that every child is created in the image of God and is precious to Him. Her love and persistence helped change attitudes in many communities. A Peacemaker Mary often settled disputes between villages and families. Because of her wisdom, fairness, and love, many local leaders respected her judgment. She became known as someone who could bring peace where there had been conflict. Her influence extended beyond preaching to helping communities live together peacefully. Challenges She Faced Mary's ministry was filled with difficulties. 1. Poverty She grew up with very little and understood hardship from childhood. 2. Dangerous Diseases She suffered from repeated attacks of malaria and other tropical illnesses. 3. Difficult Living Conditions She often lived in simple huts with few comforts. 4. Loneliness Mary never married. She devoted her life entirely to God's work and the people she served. 5. Opposition Some people resisted her message and her efforts to stop harmful practices. Yet she continued to love them and serve faithfully. Her Successes By God's grace, Mary's ministry had a lasting impact. She: Led many people to faith in Jesus Christ. Helped stop the killing and abandonment of twins in many areas. Established schools and encouraged education. Promoted peace among communities. Trained local Christian leaders. Demonstrated Christ's love through practical service. Her work opened doors for future missionaries and Nigerian church leaders. Her Final Years Even as her health declined, Mary refused to leave the people she loved. She continued serving in Nigeria until the end of her life. On January 13, 1915, Mary Slessor died in Nigeria at the age of 66. She was buried in Nigeria, the country she had come to love as her home. Her funeral was attended by both Europeans and Nigerians, reflecting the deep respect she had earned. Lessons from Mary Slessor's Life 1. Obey God's Call God can use anyone who is willing to say "yes." 2. Love People Sacrificially Mary gave her life to serve those who could never repay her. 3. Courage Overcomes Fear She entered places others feared because she trusted God. 4. Every Life Is Precious She defended vulnerable children because she believed all people are valuable to God. 5. Share the Gospel Through Words and Actions Mary preached Christ and also demonstrated His love through compassion and service. Conclusion Mary Slessor's life is a powerful testimony of faith, courage, and compassion. From a poor Scottish girl to one of the most beloved missionaries in Nigerian history, she showed that a life fully surrendered to Christ can transform countless others. Her legacy continues to inspire Christians to love the forgotten, defend the vulnerable, obey God's call without fear, and carry the Gospel to every nation.
