God's General Story
Billy Graham
The Evangelist Who Preached Christ to the World
Billy Graham was one of the most influential evangelists of the twentieth and early twenty-first centuries. For more than 60 years, he preached the Gospel of Jesus Christ to millions of people across the world, always calling them to repentance and faith in Christ. His life was marked by humility, integrity, faithfulness, and an unwavering commitment to the Bible. Early Life Billy Graham was born William Franklin Graham Jr. on November 7, 1918, near Charlotte, North Carolina. He grew up on a dairy farm where he learned the values of hard work, honesty, and responsibility. Although he attended church with his family, he was not deeply committed to Christ during his early years. His Conversion At the age of 16, Billy attended a series of revival meetings led by evangelist Mordecai Ham. During those meetings, he realized he was a sinner in need of God's grace. He gave his life to Jesus Christ, and from that day forward, his life changed dramatically. He developed a deep love for the Bible and sensed that God was calling him to preach. Preparing for Ministry Billy attended the Florida Bible Institute, where he studied the Scriptures and prepared for ministry. Later, he continued his education at Wheaton College. While at Wheaton, he met Ruth Bell, the daughter of missionary parents who had served in China. They married in 1943 and enjoyed more than fifty years of marriage. God blessed them with five children. The Beginning of His Ministry Billy first served as a pastor and later became involved with a youth ministry called Youth for Christ. His powerful preaching and clear presentation of the Gospel quickly attracted large crowds. He had one simple message: Jesus Christ died for your sins, rose again, and offers eternal life to everyone who believes in Him. The Los Angeles Crusade In 1949, Billy Graham held a series of meetings in Los Angeles, California. The meetings were originally planned for only a few weeks but were extended because of the overwhelming response. Newspapers began reporting on the meetings, and Billy Graham became known across America. This crusade launched his worldwide ministry. Billy Graham Evangelistic Association In 1950, he founded the Billy Graham Evangelistic Association. Through this ministry, he organized evangelistic crusades, produced radio and television programs, distributed Christian literature, and later expanded into internet outreach. The association continues to proclaim the Gospel around the world. Preaching Around the World Billy Graham preached in more than 185 countries and territories. He spoke in stadiums, parks, churches, and public squares. It is estimated that he preached in person to more than 210 million people, while hundreds of millions more heard his message through television, radio, books, and the internet. His sermons were simple, biblical, and always centered on Jesus Christ. A Man of Integrity Billy Graham understood that many Christian leaders had fallen because of money, pride, or immoral behavior. To protect his ministry, he and his team adopted guidelines that became known as the Modesto Manifesto. These included: Being transparent in financial matters. Avoiding situations that could lead to moral compromise. Reporting attendance honestly. Working with churches from different Christian traditions while keeping the Gospel central. His commitment to integrity earned him the respect of Christians and non-Christians alike. Personal Challenges Although greatly used by God, Billy Graham faced many trials. 1. Criticism Some criticized him for cooperating with Christians from different denominations. Others thought his message was too simple. Yet Billy remained focused on preaching Christ. 2. Physical Weakness As he grew older, he battled illnesses including Parkinson's disease, hip problems, and vision loss. Even when his strength declined, his passion for the Gospel remained. 3. Family Sacrifices His worldwide travels meant long periods away from his family. His wife Ruth faithfully supported him while caring for their children. His Successes By God's grace, Billy Graham became one of history's best-known evangelists. Some of his lasting achievements include: Preaching the Gospel on every inhabited continent. Leading millions of people to consider the claims of Christ. Founding the Billy Graham Evangelistic Association. Publishing numerous books. Helping train evangelists and Christian leaders. Providing biblical counsel to many world leaders while remaining independent of political office. His Love for the Bible Billy Graham believed the Bible was God's inspired Word. He often held up his Bible during his sermons and would say: "The Bible says..." He wanted people to place their confidence in God's Word rather than in his opinions. His Final Years Billy Graham gradually retired from public ministry because of declining health. He spent his final years quietly at his home in Montreat, North Carolina. On February 21, 2018, at the age of 99, Billy Graham went home to be with the Lord. World leaders, pastors, and millions of Christians honored his life and ministry. Lessons from Billy Graham's Life 1. Keep Jesus at the Center Billy Graham never preached himself—he preached Christ. 2. Stay Faithful to God's Word He believed the Bible should be the foundation of every sermon and every Christian life. 3. Live with Integrity Character is just as important as gifting. 4. Love People Billy preached to people of every race, culture, and social background because he believed the Gospel is for everyone. 5. Finish Well He remained faithful to his calling for more than six decades. Conclusion Billy Graham's life demonstrates how God can use one person who is completely surrendered to Him. From a young farm boy in North Carolina to a preacher who proclaimed Christ to millions around the globe, his ministry was marked by simplicity, biblical faithfulness, humility, and integrity. His greatest desire was never fame or influence—it was that people would come to know Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior. His life continues to remind believers that the Gospel is the power of God for salvation, and there is no greater calling than pointing people to Christ.
